10. COCEAN2: A SUSTAINABLE INITIATIVE FOR CARBON OFFSETTING

    Abstract
    COcean2 is a pioneering project designed to create high-quality carbon offsets by
    converting harvested freshwater macrophytes from Swiss lakes into biochar. This
    venture contributes to the circular economy leverages underutilized ecological
    resources and supports sustainable agriculture by reducing the need for chemical
    fertilizers.

11. Introduction

    In Swiss lakes special authorizations allow communes to harvest macrophytes to facilitate
    access to ports and swimming areas. These activities provide a limited yet significant source of
    raw materials for the COcean2 project aligning with our goal to convert macrophytes into
    beneficial biochar while adhering to environmental regulations.

12. Market Analysis

    Recent communications with local authorities indicate an annual yield of 300 to 600 tons of 
    macrop hytes subject to meteorological conditions. Current practices involve composting or 
    using the harv ested biomass as fertilizer which presents COcean2 with an opportunity to 
    introduce biochar as a sustainable alternative. 

13. Sustainability and Impact

    The project aims to transform a portion of the 1400 tons of wet macrophyte biomass
    typically used for biogas or compost into high-quality biochar. This shift not only supports
    sustainable agriculture but also aligns with Swiss environmental goals by providing a value-
    added product from otherwise problematic plant growth.

14. Technical Setup and Pilot Project

    Considering the variable nature of biomass availability due to weather conditions our pilot 
    project will include flexible operational plans to adapt to these variations. We will engage with 
    local communit ies and farmers building relationships to potentially redirect some biomass 
    from current biogas or composting uses to biochar production.

15. Regulatory Considerations

    Navigating the regulatory landscape is crucial for COcean2. We will work closely with environmental
    authorities to secure necessary permits for harvesting macrophytes ensuring compliance with Swiss
    environmental protection laws and contributing to the responsible management of natural resources.

16. Conclusion

    COcean2’s approach is informed by an initial market research aligning with current environmental
    practices and addressing the need for innovative sustainable agricultural inputs. By transforming
    macrophytes into biochar our aim is to create an eco-friendly solution to the management of
    nutrients in agriculture support Swiss goals of carbon neutrality and promote a circular economy.

18. Brief description of COCean2: 

    The idea is to create a project that will generate high-quality carbon offsets from the
    residual macrophytes left over from mowing lakes in Switzerland by producing
    biochar from these macrophytes. Somewhat inspired by this scientific article: 
    https://academica-
    e.unavarra.es/bitstream/handle/2454/44665/Poveda_UseFreshwater_1674654771276_
    42195.pdf?sequence=1&isAllowed=y . COcean2's mission is to accelerate carbon
    offsetting from marine projects. For context there's nothing concrete yet it's just 2
    young EPFL graduates looking to develop their project with the support of our
    institutions (E4S and UNIL's entrepreneurial HUB as well as the support of other
    people in the sustainability field in Lausanne).

    Here's why they think it could be a good project: "Many freshwater macrophytes are
    widely used in the phytoremediation of water contaminated by heavy metals and
    other contaminants. The use of the biomass formed by these macrophytes as an input
    for agriculture requires exhaustive studies on the flow of pollutants to soil and crops. 
    In environmental terms the use of freshwater macrophytes as an agricultural
    resource implies the development of a circular economy linked to the need to remove
    these plants from various ecosystems. What's more thanks to the nutrient
    contribution they make to crops when applied in the field and the absorption of
    leached nutrients when they grow in bodies of water their use in agriculture
    represents a significant reduction in the use of chemical fertilizers. In addition it is
    important to emphasize the role that the use of freshwater macrophytes can play in
    achieving carbon neutrality on a global scale. These plants can help mitigate climate
    change by storing carbon as they grow and then be used as a resource in agriculture."
